Athena

Land-based and somacultural therapy (Indigenous-focused)

Areas of focus / Approach

I strive to be person-centered, supporting clients in their knowing, power, and strengths by including land-based, cultural-based, and somatic Indigenous practices. Decolonizing work is carried out in collaboration with clients as they explore the role of culture and community in their lives. This involves discovering how one is personally affected by colonization by connecting with the intersections of identity, exploring how culture shapes the sense of self at the individual, relational, and community levels, and how cultural understandings influence how one feels in one's body.

As a soma-cultural therapist, I provide specialized services, including nature and land-based therapy, facilitating intentional, experiential-based activities to help meet the client's therapeutic goals. These services include office-based therapy to arrange outdoor therapy activities, urban adventures such as walk-and-talk sessions, and cultural land-based activities centered around reconnecting with the land and Indigenous teachings. Additional services are available for individuals and families seeking therapy related to the impact of Missing and Murdered Indigenous Relatives (MMIR). I can also assist with grief and loss and provide support for abuse survivors, including those affected by residential and day schools, from culturally appropriate therapeutic approaches.
