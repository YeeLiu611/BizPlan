Buddhist Psychological Counseling & Buddhist Counseling

Areas of focus / Approach

I offer Buddhist Psychological Counseling, which merges contemporary psychotherapeutic strategies with key Buddhist concepts — such as impermanence, compassion, and mindfulness — to address emotional and mental challenges within a clinically oriented structure. By integrating evidence-based techniques, I guide individuals toward greater self-awareness, symptom relief, and improved psychological well-being, all while drawing upon Buddhist insights to illuminate the nature of suffering. This fusion allows clients to develop deeper acceptance and resilience in facing life's adversities without necessarily adopting a strictly religious or monastic framework.

I also offer Buddhist Counseling, which emphasizes traditional Buddhist teachings and spiritual cultivation, often facilitated by monks, nuns, or devoted lay practitioners who serve as spiritual mentors rather than clinically trained professionals. In this faith-based environment, the focus shifts toward ethical conduct, meditation, and understanding karma, with the ultimate aim of reducing suffering through spiritual growth and progress on the path to enlightenment. Sessions may include teaching, chanting, scripture study, Zen practice, and moral reflections, underscoring the importance of long-term practice and devotion.

While both approaches strive to alleviate distress and foster well-being, Buddhist Psychological Counseling operates under modern mental health paradigms, whereas Buddhist Counseling is rooted in traditional Buddhist doctrine and praxis.
